## The problem

Post-harvest loss is one of the most expensive, least visible problems in African
agriculture. A meaningful share of what smallholder farmers harvest never reaches
a paying buyer in good condition — lost to delayed sales, poor price information,
and disconnected logistics. The loss doesn't happen in the field; it happens in
the gap between harvest and market.

## The hackathon

HarvestFlow began as an entry to the **Drones for Africa Hackathon 2025–2026**,
where it placed **2nd**. The brief called for drone-informed applications in
agriculture; HarvestFlow's response was to treat drone-derived yield and
condition data as one input into a larger problem — getting that harvest sold,
priced fairly, and moved before it loses value.

The prototype was strong enough to be presented directly to **Honourable
Minister Tiroyaone Ntsima**, turning a hackathon weekend into a conversation
about real deployment.

## What HarvestFlow does

<Cards>
  <Card title="Marketplace" description="Connects smallholder farmers directly to institutional and bulk buyers, removing layers of informal middlemen that compress farmer margins." />
  <Card title="Real-Time Pricing" description="Surfaces live market price signals so farmers can negotiate and time sales using data instead of guesswork." />
  <Card title="Logistics Coordination" description="Matches transport and storage to harvested produce, shrinking the window where value degrades after harvest." />
</Cards>

## Where it stands now

HarvestFlow has moved beyond the hackathon stage and is in active development
as a startup. The current focus is hardening the three pillars above into a
usable product — starting with the marketplace and pricing layers, with
logistics coordination following as pilot partnerships are confirmed.

## Why this project

This case study sits at the intersection of the two things this portfolio is
built around: software and finance. HarvestFlow is, underneath the product
language, a market-design problem — getting price information and transaction
flow to move correctly between parties who don't currently have access to
either. That's the part worth building well.
